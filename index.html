About us

We are a family run business with roots in Knoxville, Tennessee. The ranch is led by Marcos Alonso, who brings many years of experience raising cattle and a B.S. in Animal Science from Texas A&M. Our family performs all of the day to day work on the ranch, and we feel privileged to bring premium Black Angus beef to your table at a price that stays fair.

How we raise

Our herd is built on premium Black Angus genetics. Cattle graze pastures we keep free of pesticides, with plenty of grass and grain as part of a balanced feeding program. We farm with ethics and regenerative practices in mind, caring for the soil and giving our animals a calm, healthy life.

What we sell

We are growing thoughtfully, so availability is limited for now. To keep things fair for neighbors and returning customers, we currently limit each order to 60 pounds of beef. That helps more families enjoy our beef while the quality stays consistent. Our price sheet lists cuts, bundles, and what we have available today.

Pickup & delivery

We currently do not ship beef. When you buy from us, we will coordinate pickup or local delivery together so it fits your schedule and ours. Just ask when you place your order.

A deeper dive into regenerative practices and why they matter

You do not need a ranching background to understand how we work. Open the section below for plain language on breeding, land stewardship, and one surprising way we fight flies without extra chemicals.

Breeding choices, land care, and natural fly control Tap to expand the full story

At Colibri Land & Cattle, we believe great beef starts long before the grill. A big part of that is how we breed cattle. We use artificial insemination, which means we pair each cow with specific genetics on purpose instead of leaving mating to chance. That lets us raise animals that grow well on feed, carry less waste for the resources we use, and still deliver the eating qualities people notice at the table.

In everyday terms, we are aiming for cattle that convert grass and grain into beef efficiently, with a lighter footprint where we can, while still producing rich marbling, tender cuts, and a generous ribeye. Those traits are not buzzwords to us—they are the measurable outcomes we select for when we choose genetics.

We also use sexed genetics, which is a way to stack the odds toward calves of the sex we want for each role on the ranch. We raise high performing steers for beef, and we build strong replacement females for the cow herd so the next generation stays healthy and consistent. That discipline is how we keep quality steady over time instead of riding luck.

All of that sits inside a bigger idea: regenerative ranching—taking care of soil, water, and pasture so the land can keep supporting cattle and wildlife for decades. Stewardship is not a side project here. It is part of how we think about the whole operation.

One practical piece you might not expect is fly control. Flies stress cattle and push people toward heavier pesticide use. We use tiny beneficial parasitic wasps that target fly eggs in the environment. They are a natural check on fly numbers, which helps us spray less and gives our cattle a calmer, healthier place to live.
